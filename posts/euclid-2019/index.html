1a

Joyce has two identical jars. The first jar is 3/4 full of water and contains 300 mL of water. The second jar is 1/4 full of water. How much water, in mL, does the second jar contain?

$$ \frac{3}{4} \text{water} = 300mL $$ $$ \frac{1}{4} = 100mL $$ $$ \therefore \text{The second jar has 100mL of water} $$

1b

What integer a satisfies 3 < 24/a < 4 ?

$$ 3 \lt \frac{24}{a} \lt 4 $$ $$ \text{Note that a must be a positive integer} $$ $$ \because a > 0 \therefore 3a \lt 24 \lt 4a $$ $$ 3a \lt 24 \implies a \lt 8 \text{ and } 24 \lt 4a \implies 6 \lt a$$ $$ \because 6 \lt a \lt 8 \text { and } a \in \N \therefore a = 7 $$

1c

If 1/x^2 - 1/x = 2, determine all possible values of x.

$$ \frac{1}{x^2} - \frac{1}{x} = 2 ; x \neq 0 $$ $$ 1 - x = 2x^2 $$ $$ 0 = 2x^2 + x - 1 $$ $$ 0 = (2x-1)(x+1) $$ $$ \therefore x = -1 \text{ or } x = \frac{1}{2} $$

2a

In the diagram, two small circles of radius 1 are tangent to each other and to a larger circle of radius 2. What is the area of the shaded region?

$$ A_\text{shaded} = A_\text{large circle} - 2A_\text{small circle} $$ $$ = \pi(2)^2 - 2\pi(1)^2 $$ $$ = 2\pi $$

2b

Kari jogs at a constant speed of 8 km/h. Mo jogs at a constant speed of 6 km/h. Kari and Mo jog from the same starting point to the same finishing point along a straight road. Mo starts at 10:00 a.m. Kari and Mo both finish at 11:00 a.m. At what time did Kari start to jog?

$$ s_K = 8km/h $$ $$ s_M = 6km/h $$

$$ \text{Using time/distance/speed formulas, this can be expressed} $$ $$ \Delta d_K = s_K\Delta t_K $$ $$ \Delta d_M = s_M\Delta t_M $$ $$ \because \text{The two ran the same distance } \therefore s_K\Delta t_K = s_M\Delta t_M $$ $$ \Delta t_K = \frac{s_M\Delta t_M}{s_K} $$ $$ \text{Knowing that Mo took 1 hour to jog from 10 am to 11 am} $$ $$\Delta t_K = \frac{6km/h \times 1h}{8km/h} $$ $$\Delta t_K = \frac{3}{4}h $$ $$\therefore \text{Kari took 45 minutes to run the same distance, and thus, started at 10:15am} $$

2c

The line with equation x+ 3y = 7 is parallel to the line with equation y = mx+b. The line with equation y = mx + b passes through the point (9, 2). Determine the value of b.

$$ \text {The slope of the equation y=mx+b must have the same slope as x+3y=7 for it to be parallel} $$ $$ \text{Thus, determine the slope of x+3y = 7 by rearranging it to slope-intercept form: } y = \frac{-1}{3}x + \frac{7}{3}$$

$$ m = \frac{-1}{3} $$ $$ \text{Thus, } y = \frac{-1}{3}x + b$$ $$ \text{Sub (9,2) for (x,y)} $$ $$ 2 = \frac{-9}{3} + b $$ $$ \therefore b = 5$$

3a

Michelle calculates the average of the following numbers: 5, 10, 15, 16, 24, 28, 33, 37. Daphne removes one number and calculates the average of the remaining numbers. The average that Daphne calculates is one less than the average that Michelle calculates. Which number does Daphne remove?

$$ Avg_\text{Michelle} = \frac{5 + 10 + … + 37}{8} = 21 $$ $$ \text{Thus, } Avg_\text{Daphne} = 20 $$ $$ \because \text{Daphne removes 1 number from the set, the total sum she added up before dividing is } 20 \times 7 = 140 $$ $$ \text{The sum Michelle added up is 168} $$ $$168 - 140 = 28 \therefore \text{ Daphne removed the number 28 from Michelle’s set} $$

3b

If 16 ^ (15/x) = 32 ^(4/3), what is the value of x? $$\text{Rewrite into the same base (which is 2)} $$ $$2^{4}{^{\frac{15}{x}}} = 2^{5}{^{\frac{4}{3}}}$$ $$ \because \text{both sides have the same base} \therefore \frac{60}{x} = \frac{20}{3} $$ $$ \therefore x = 9 $$

3c

Suppose that (2^2022 + 2^a) / 2^2019 = 72. Determine the value of a $$ \frac{2^{2022} + 2^a}{2^{2019}} = 72 $$ $$ \text{For simplicity, let } b = 2^{2019} $$ $$ \frac{2^3b + 2^a}{b} = 72$$ $$ 8b + 2^a = 72b $$ $$ 2^a = 64b $$ $$ 2^a = 2^6 \times 2^{2019} $$ $$ 2^a = 2^{2025} $$ $$ \therefore a = 2025 $$

4a

In the diagram, triangle ABC has a right angle at B and point D lies on AB. If DB = 10, ∠ACD = 30◦ and ∠CDB = 60◦, what is the length of AD?

$$ \text{Note that } \triangle CDB \text{ is a 30-60-90 triangle, and thus, } CB = 10\sqrt3$$ $$ \text{Note that } \triangle ABC \text { is also a 30-60-90 triangle, and thus } AB = 10\sqrt3\sqrt3 = 30$$ $$ AD = AB - DB $$ $$ AD = 30 - 10 $$ $$\therefore AD = 20 $$

4b

The points A(d, −d) and B(−d + 12, 2d − 6) both lie on a circle centered at the origin. Determine the possible values of d.

$$ \text{Knowing that } x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \text { since it is a circle about the origin } $$ $$ r^2 = d^2 + (-d)^2 \text{ and } r^2 = (-d+12)^2 + (2d-6)^2 $$ $$ 2d^2 = d^2 -24d + 144 + 4d^2 - 24d + 36 $$ $$ 0 = 3d^2 - 48d + 180 $$ $$ 0 = d^2 - 16d + 60 $$ $$ 0 = (d-10)(d-6) $$ $$ \therefore d = 6 \text { or } d = 10 $$

5a

Determine the two pairs of positive integers (a, b) with a < b that satisfy the equation √a + √b = √50

$$ \sqrt a + \sqrt b = \sqrt{50} $$ $$ \sqrt b = \sqrt{50} - \sqrt a $$ $$ b = 50 + a - 2\sqrt{50a} $$ $$ b = 50 + a - 2(5)\sqrt{2a} $$ $$ b = 50 + a - 10\sqrt{2a} $$ $$ \because b \in \N \therefore a \in \{2, 2^3, 2^5, …,2^{2n-1}\} $$ $$ \text{Iterate through the list to find valid values for b} $$ $$ (a = 2, b = 32) \cup (a = 8, b = 18) $$ $$ \text{ Note that when } a \geq 2^5, a > b. $$ $$\text{ Thus, other squares that can be substituted for a (e.g., 4, 9, 16, etc) will not work after being multiplied by 2 to eleminate the radical }$$ $$ \therefore (2, 32) \cup (8, 18) $$

5b

Consider the system of equations: c + d = 2000; c/d = k Determine the number of integers k with k ≥ 0 for which there is at least one pair of integers (c, d) that is a solution to the system.

$$ \text{Rearrange both equations in terms of d} $$ $$ c = 2000 - d \text{ and } c = kd $$ $$ \text{Thus, } 2000-d = kd $$ $$ 2000 = kd + d $$ $$ 2000 = d(k + 1) $$ $$ \frac{2000}{k+1} = d $$ $$ \because k \geq 0 \text{ and } d \in \Z \therefore k+1 | 2000 \text { and } k+1 \geq 1$$ $$ \text{Furthermore } k+1 | 2000 \text { and } k+1 \geq 1 \implies d \in \N \implies c \in \N $$ $$ \text{Thus, determine the number of unique factors of 2000, using prime factorization }$$ $$ 2000 = 2^4 \times 5^3 \text{ which means there are } 5 \times 4 = 20 \text{ possible factors that are positive for } k+1 $$ $$ \therefore \text{There are 20 possible solutions, and thus, 20 different values for k} $$

6a

A regular pentagon covers part of another regular polygon, as shown. This regular polygon has n sides, five of which are completely or partially visible. In the diagram, the sum of the measures of the angles marked a◦ and b◦ is 88◦. Determine the value of n. (The side lengths of a regular polygon are all equal, as are the measures of its interior angles.)

$$\text{Note that the sum of interior angles of any n degree polygon (regular or irregular) is } 180\degree (n - 2) $$

$$ \text{Looking at the 7 sided polygon here, the sum of its interior angles is } 180\degree (7-2) = 900 \degree $$ $$ \text{Let x represent the interior angle of the unknown regular polygon} $$ $$ 900 \degree = a \degree + b \degree + 4x \degree \text { and } a \degree + b \degree = 88 \degree$$ $$ 4x \degree = 560 \degree \implies x = 140 \degree$$ $$ \because \text{The interior angle is } 140 \degree \therefore \frac{180 \degree (n - 2)}{n} = 140\degree \text{ where n represents the number of sides the unknown polygon has} $$ $$ \therefore n = 9 $$

6b

In trapezoid ABCD, BC is parallel to AD and BC is perpendicular to AB. Also, the lengths of AD, AB and BC, in that order, form a geometric sequence. Prove that AC is perpendicular to BD. (A geometric sequence is a sequence in which each term after the first is obtained from the previous term by multiplying it by a non-zero constant.)

$$\text{An easy way to prove that line segment AC and BD are perpendicular is to plot the trapezoid on a cartesian plane}$$ $$\text{Let } |\overline{AD}| = a, |\overline{AB}| = ar, |\overline{BC}| = ka^2$$ $$\text{And then plot the trapezoid like so}$$

$$\text{To prove that AC and BD are perpendicular, compare the slopes of line AC and BD to see if they are negative reciprocals of each other}$$ $$m_{AC} = \frac{-ar}{ar^2} = \frac{-1}{r}$$ $$m_{BD} = \frac{ar}{a} = r$$ $$\because m_{AC} = \frac{-1}{m_{BD}} \therefore \overline{AC} \text{ and } \overline{BD} \text{ are perpendicular}$$

7a

Determine all real numbers x for which 2 log_{2}(x − 1) = 1 − log_{2}(x + 2).

$$ \text{ The logarithmic function on both sides is not defined for values less than 0} $$ $$ \text{Thus, }\because x-1 > 0 \text{ or } x+2 > 0 \therefore x > 1 $$

$$\text{Using log and exponent laws, the equation can be rearranged like so}$$ $$2\log_2(x-1) = 1 - \log_2(x+2)$$ $$\log_2[(x-1)^2] + \log_2(x+2) = 1$$ $$\log_2((x^2 - 2x + 1)(x+2)) = 1$$ $$\log_2(x^3 + 2x^2 - 2x^2 - 4x + x + 2) = 1$$ $$2^1 = x^3 - 3x + 2$$ $$0 = x^3 - 3x$$ $$0 = x(x^2 - 3)$$ $$ x = 0 \text{ or } x = \pm\sqrt{3}$$ $$\because x > 1 \therefore \text{ the only possible value for x is } \sqrt{3} $$

7b

Consider the function f(x) = x^2 − 2x. Determine all real numbers x that satisfy the equation f(f(f(x))) = 3

$$ \text{Let a } = f(f(x))$$ $$ f(a) = 3 $$ $$ a^2 - 2a - 3 = 0$$ $$ (a-3)(a+1) = 0$$ $$ a = 3 \text { or } a = -1$$ $$f(f(x)) = 3 \text{ or } f(f(x)) = -1$$ $$ \text{Let b = } f(x) $$ $$f(b) = 3 \text{ or } f(b) = -1$$ $$b^2 - 2b - 3 = 0 \text{ or } b^2 - 2b + 1 = 0$$ $$(b - 3)(b + 1) = 0 \text{ or } (b-1)^2 = 0$$ $$b = 3 \text{ or } b = -1 \text{ or } b = 1$$ $$f(x) = 3 \text{ or } f(x) = -1 \text{ or } f(x) = 1$$ $$x^2 - 2x - 3 = 0 \text{ or } x^2 - 2x + 1 = 0 \text{ or } x^2 - 2x -1 = 0$$ $$\text{Solving for the three quadratic equations, } x = 3 \text { or } x = \pm1 \text{ or } x = 1 \pm \sqrt2$$

8a

A circle has centre O and radius 1. Quadrilateral ABCD has all 4 sides tangent to the circle at points P, Q, S, and T, as shown. Also, ∠AOB = ∠BOC = ∠COD = ∠DOA. If AO = 3, determine the length of DS. $$ \because \angle{AOB} =\angle{BOC} = \angle{COD} = \angle{DOA} \text{ and form a circle } \therefore \text{ they are all } 90\degree $$ $$\text{Thus, the quadrilateral is a parallelogram shape where } \triangle{AOB}, \triangle{BOC}, \triangle{COD}, \triangle{DOA} \text{ are identical} $$

$$\text{Therefore, the diagram can be simplified and focus on either of the four triangles.}$$ $$\text{For example, using triangle DOA, where the length of TD = DS, the length of OT = 1, and the length of AO = 3}$$ $$\because \angle{OTD} = \angle{ATO} \text{ and } \angle{TDO} = \angle{TOA} \therefore\triangle{OTD} \cong \triangle{ATO} $$ $$ \frac{|\overline{TD}|}{|\overline{TO}|} = \frac{|\overline{TO}|}{|\overline{TA}|}$$ $$ |\overline{TA}| = \sqrt{|\overline{TO}|^2 + |\overline{AO^2}|}$$ $$ |\overline{TA}| = \sqrt{1^2 + 3^2} = 2\sqrt{2}$$ $$|\overline{TA}| = \frac{|\overline{TO}|^2}{|\overline{TA}|} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt2}$$ $$\therefore |\overline{DS}| = \frac{1}{2\sqrt2}$$

8b

Suppose that x satisfies 0 < x < π/2 and cos(3/2 cos x) = sin(3/2 sin x). Determine all possible values of sin2x, expressing your answers in the form (aπ^2 + bπ + c) / d where a, b, c, d are integers.

$$ 0 \lt x \lt \frac{\pi}{2} \iff 0 \lt \cos x \lt 1 \implies 0 \lt \frac{3}{2}\cos x \lt \frac{3}{2}$$ $$ 0 \lt x \lt \frac{\pi}{2} \iff 0 \lt \sin x \lt 1 \implies 0 \lt \frac{3}{2}\sin x \lt \frac{3}{2}$$

$$\text{Let } \frac{3}{2}\cos x \text{ and } \frac{3}{2}\sin x \text{ be a and b respectively} $$ $$ 0 \lt a \lt \frac{3}{2} \text{ and } 0 \lt b \lt \frac{3}{2} $$ $$ \cos a = \sin b $$ $$\text{Note that when a triangle is inscribed in a unit circle, cos a represents the height of the triangle and sin b represents the width of the triangle.}$$ $$\text{When cos a = sin b, the triangle is a right-angled isosceles where a = b = } \frac{\pi}{4}$$ $$ \text{Note that } \frac{\pi}{4} \lt \frac{3}{2}$$ $$\therefore a + b = \frac{\pi}{2}$$ $$\frac{3}{2}\cos x + \frac{3}{2}\sin x = \frac{\pi}{2}$$ $$\cos x + \sin x = \frac{\pi}{3}$$ $$\cos^2 x + 2\cos x\sin x + \sin^2 x = \frac{\pi^2}{9}$$ $$2\cos x\sin x = \frac{\pi^2 - 9}{9}$$ $$\therefore \sin 2x = \frac{\pi^2 - 9}{9}$$
